What do we do?

Since 1990 the Aran valley has an avalanche technician specifically. Since the 2003/2004 season, with the incorporation of a second technician, the monitoring and local Avalanche forecast for the roads was launched, as well as the first avalanche bulletins for mountaineers produced and issued from the territory of Aran itself. In the 2010/2011 season expands the team with a third component, with a more scientific profile, with the idea of promoting applied research.

This culminates in the creation of the Aran Avalanche Center, located in the CEI of Casau village.

Today, the main activities developed in the centre are:

  • Avalanche forecast for mountaineers: Avalanche Danger Bulletin issued daily at 5:00 p.m., and updated at 8:00 a.m. if necessary.
  • Forecast and control of avalanches for access roads 142-b to Beret and C-28 of Port de la Bonaigua: Avalanche Danger Report addressed to road managers. Avalanche control with the Daisybell system to mitigate the danger.
  • Avalanche mapping: recordin of avalanche events for the avalanche database in Catalonia (accidental avalanches, natural avalanches in vulnerable areas, large and very large avalanches)
  • Cartography of avalanche terrain based on the Avalanche Terrain Exposure Scale (ATES).
  • Applied research on snow and avalanches. For example: analysis and characterization of accidents and human triggered slabs; study of areas vulnerable to avalanches.

As members of the European Avalanche Warning Services (EAWS), in recent years we have concentrated efforts to incorporate the EAWS standards into the avalanche bulletin, so that it is harmonious with the rest of the services. In addition, since the 2020/2021 season, we have adopted the Albina platform that allows us to produce a multilingual newsletter with more appropriate broadcast times for trip planning and checking the bulletin before going out.
